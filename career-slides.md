## Introduction
- AI developed from 1960s–1980s  
- Began impacting the justice system in the late 2010s  
- Focus: positive and negative impacts in the legal field  

---

## What Type of AI is Affecting This?
### LLM Models
- Used in forensics, criminology, and social work  
- Generate human-like text and communication  
- Examples: ChatGPT, Gemini, Meta AI  

---

## AI Benefits
- Speeds up document production in courts  
- Enhances data analysis and pattern recognition  
- Helps identify neural networks and trends  

---

## AI Disruption
- Increase in cybersecurity crimes  
- Risk of false evaluations (motives, mental health)  
- Cannot fully understand human emotion or empathy  
- Bias concerns  

---

## Case Study: Technique of Neutralization (ToN)
- Used to explain how criminals justify actions  
- Denial of the victim appears in ~70% of cases  
- AI can help analyze patterns, but struggles vs human judgment  

---

## Gaining an Advantage
- Use AI tools for data analysis  
- Improve efficiency in case preparation  
- Understand AI limitations in legal interpretation  

---

## Action Plan
- Study AI use in criminal justice  
- Practice using AI tools  
- Stay informed on ethics and regulations  

---

## Final Takeaway
- AI improves efficiency but cannot replace human judgment  
- Responsible use is essential in legal fields
