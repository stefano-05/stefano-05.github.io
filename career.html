AI and Its Effect on the Criminal Justice System

Career Overview

The creation of AI will impact everything and everyone in some shape or form, the good, better, and the bad. “AI-Enabled Future Crime,” by authors Caldwell, Andrews, Tanay, and Griffin, is a review of LLMs and their use in law, as well as a study of AI- and cybercrimes committed by perpetrators. Additionally, the “Possibility of Using Publicly Available Neural Networks in Criminal Proceedings” by Spiridonov argues more negatively on AI and its failure to comprehend human motives and emotions, and the skyrocketing rates of many different types of cyber crimes. The introduction of AI into the Criminology and Forensic Science profession will enhance analytical capabilities. However, it will remain crucial to retain human judgment in matters of interpretation and legality.

AI Benefits

Initially, AI can help enhance data analysis and structure. The authors note that most AI systems are designed to solve specific tasks rather than operate as fully general intelligence. AI allows criminologists and forensic scientists to handle workloads more efficiently and improves communication across legal teams. It can also identify patterns in large datasets, which is essential for solving crimes.

Spiridonov (2026) supports the idea that AI can be useful in identifying neural networks and patterns when used appropriately. AI is most effective when assisting with organization, research, and procedural guidance rather than replacing human judgment.

AI Disruption

Despite its advantages, AI introduces serious risks. It struggles to understand human emotions, motives, and empathy, which are critical in legal decision-making. AI systems can also make errors in interpreting laws, sentencing, and determining criminal behavior.

Additionally, AI contributes to increasing cybercrime. It can be used to generate fake content, exploit vulnerabilities, and assist in criminal activity. These risks highlight the need for strong human oversight.

Gaining an Advantage

Someone entering criminology or forensic science can gain an advantage by learning how to use AI responsibly. This includes using AI tools for data analysis, improving efficiency in case preparation, and understanding the limitations of AI in legal interpretation.

Action Plan

  • Learn how AI is used in criminal justice and forensic science
  • Practice using AI tools for data analysis
  • Stay informed on ethical guidelines and legal restrictions
